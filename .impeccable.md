# Design Context

## Target Audience

Victor Bona's blog is primarily for engineering peers, senior builders, founders, and operators who want to evaluate how he thinks and what he has shipped.

Readers should quickly understand:

- Which technical ideas Victor is currently arguing for.
- Which real systems back up those ideas.
- Which projects, papers, and long-form work are worth following.

## Use Cases

The site should help readers:

- Scan recent and important technical writing without wading through a tall homepage.
- Evaluate Victor's engineering taste through shipped systems such as Guara Cloud, Purple Wolf, and SQLTemple.
- Navigate quickly between writing, projects, and papers.
- Read posts comfortably in both light and dark mode.
- Treat older writing as an archive without making the site feel stale.

## Brand Personality

The direction is **Operating Index with controlled Technical Zine edges**.

The interface should feel:

- Minimal, raw, and opinionated.
- Content-first and dense, without becoming visually overloaded.
- Serious and practical, with projects as proof behind the writing.
- Sharp in wording, but not loud or performative.

## Visual Constraints

- Preserve automatic light/dark support and make both modes intentional.
- Avoid excessive verticality; first viewport should expose author signal, a featured idea, recent writing, and the start of shipped systems.
- Prefer rows, compact metadata, hard dividers, and typographic hierarchy over tall card grids.
- Avoid gradient text, thick side-stripe accents, glow-heavy dark UI, generic icon-card grids, nested cards, and oversized marketing heroes.
- Use a distinctive heading/body type pairing rather than the current generic Geist-only feel.
- Keep color restrained: tinted neutrals, one sparse accent, no neon or purple-blue gradient default.
